Pursuing universe with a book - May 1, 2021

About 66magg

66magg is a book blog created for any bookworm who would like to exchange opinions and thoughts on books. The name of the blog might be quite random. However, it carries some meaning, though not the most obvious one.

In each of my day I am driven to find at least three reasons to be happy and to be thankful. However, there are tons of reasons for one to appreciate each day. Namely, the name of the blog consists of two parts: "66" meaning number of reasons and the word "magg" meaning "to love" in German in the first person form ("Ich mag"). And as a result, we have "66 reasons to be happy in a day" or "66 reasons to be appreciate each day".

Details

In this blog, you can find book-reviews, book-recommendations, music (mainly: R&B, Jazz, Rock, Pop, K-pop, Indie-folk), and last but not least, you can also share your writings or book-analysis by sending your works by email in contact page. This is a little platform for book-lovers with any experience. So, I welcome everyone!

Additionally, with the following link you can join the telegram group chat where you can find a small collection of electronical books. The collection is to be get more and more rich with the best ever books in the coming times. So, please, stay tuned.

“Martin Eden”

J. London

Künstlerroman

This book is a semi-autobiographical story which illustrates the whole way of becoming a writer of a young seaman. Through constant hard work and through varied limitations to himself in money, food, and sleep, Martin eventually reaches the realization of his dream. But what is awaiting him? Satisfaction or endless search which converges to depression?

“The Da Vinci Code”

D. Brown

Mystery, detective fiction

The 689-page long story which reveals the mysterious death of the curator of Louvre museum in France. This book has many references to the religious topics. Namely, it provides another interpretation of Holy Grail and many other things related to Christianity.

“Becoming”

M. Obama

Bibliography, memoir

Maybe, one of the best auto-bibliographical works which provides a lot of inspiration and courage to everyone and especially to minority groups. Being the first black first-lady of the USA, Michelle Obama has her unique and very touching memoirs. The way of narrating is reader friendly as if she is referring to each of her reader as she would refer to her true friend.
